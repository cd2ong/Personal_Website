WHY PURSUE UX & UI DESIGN WITH AN ENGINEERING DEGREE?

The reason that I apply to UX/UI design jobs is because the Systems Design Engineering program at the University of Waterloo focuses on much more than your typical STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) program.

Systems Design focuses on a more holistic view of engineering which incorporates a user centered design process. Over my five year degree, I was given many opportunities in the work force and in class projects to develop my UX/UI skills. This exposure nurtured my passion for UX/UI development and that is why I am choosing to pursue this field of work.

WHAT IS SYSTEMS DESIGN ENGINEERING?

Going back to my 101 class in my first term at the University of Waterloo, the instructor told us the story of "the blind men and the elephant" ( See the Wikipedia page ). In the story it is explained that multiple blind men examined different parts of an elephant. Each blind man comes to a different conclusion on what the elephant based upon what they inspected.

The instructor then proceeded to explain that Systems Design Engineering was a broad engineering discipline that took a holistic approach. Instead of examining each part separately, Systems Design looks at the big picture (the whole elephant) and how the various parts interact. This approach was ideal to learning valuable skills in full stack development and gave me the skills to communicate effectively with various demographics (everyone from engineers to programmers to graphic designers.)
